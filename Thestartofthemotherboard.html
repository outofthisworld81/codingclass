The start of the motherboard

The motherboard, also known as the mainboard or system board, serves as the central and foundational component of a computer system. It is a large, flat circuit board that acts as a hub, connecting various hardware components such as the CPU, RAM, storage devices, and expansion cards. The motherboard provides the necessary pathways for data transfer and power distribution, effectively enabling communication and coordination among all the computer's parts, making it the essential starting point for building and operating a computer.
